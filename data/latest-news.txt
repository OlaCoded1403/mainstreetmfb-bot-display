POST: MAINSTREET MFB BOARD, MANAGEMENT RETREAT AND AWARDS
URL: https://www.mainstreetmfb.com/mainstreet-mfb-management-retreat-and-awards/
Date: March 15, 2022
Category: Events & Gallery, News
Mainstreet Microfinance Bank retreat was set to look at emerging challenges and opportunities in microlending, deepen growth of the microfinance banking sector and award the best Mainstreet Microfinance Bank branch 2021.
The Board and Management Retreat is held every year to reflect on underlying issues, discuss achievable solutions and reward branch managers for recorded success.
Guests/speakers included Dr Ayo Teriba, Mrs Biodun Adedipe, Mr Olayinka Adebiyi and Mr Godwin Ehigiamusoe.
The retreat began at 10.00am with opening speech and introduction by Mr Adegoke E. Adegbami, MD/CEO, followed by a lecture by Mrs Biodun Adedipe on the global microfinance market.
Dr Godwin Ehigiamusoe spoke on risk and uncertainty in the finance sector.
Dr Ayo Teriba gave strategic insights on Nigeria's economic outlook 2022.
Mr Olayinka Olukoya Adebiyi discussed digital projects Mainstreet MFB should consider.
Top branches rewarded for 2021 included Epe Branch, Lekki Branch and Broad Street Branch.
The event ended with a closing remark from the MD and prayer by Mr Olanrewaju Waju Wilhelm, Head, HCM/Admin.

POST: OUR CREDIT RATING HAS BOOSTED CUSTOMERS’ CONFIDENCE IN OUR BANK, SAYS ADEGBAMI
URL: https://www.mainstreetmfb.com/our-credit-rating-has-boosted-customers-confidence-ln-our-bank-says-adegbami/
Date: June 10, 2021
Category: Interview
Adegoke Akinola Adegbami, Managing Director/CEO of Mainstreet Microfinance Bank, discussed the bank's debt product, Main Treasure Note, which he described as similar to commercial paper and launched in 2018.
He stated that the product helped the bank raise funds from middle-class, high-net-worth individuals and corporate entities, with subscriptions from N5 million and above, and that Agusto & Co's investment grade credit rating boosted confidence.
He said the product portfolio had grown to over N8 billion, equivalent to about $20 million at N400/$.
He discussed liquidity management, recapitalisation of microfinance banks, 2020 performance, awards, AfCFTA, SME support, bond raising, and the CBN restriction on cryptocurrency transactions.

POST: ADEGBAMI: MICROFINANCE BANKS REQUIRE GOVERNMENT SUPPORT TO HELP SMEs, LOW INCOME EARNERS
URL: https://www.mainstreetmfb.com/adegbami-microfinance-banks-require-government-support-to-help-smes-low-income-earners/
Date: December 7, 2020
Category: Interview
In this interview, Adegbami said he joined the bank in 2009 and that the bank operates with a State licence, with branches across Lagos State.
He discussed the COVID-19 impact on microfinance banks, palliative measures, CBN capital requirements, the Global Standing Instruction, the challenges facing microfinance banks and the need for government support.
He stated that Mainstreet MFB was prepared for GSI because it had been with NIBSS for more than two years.
He argued that SME lending in Nigeria is risky and that government support is needed for microfinance institutions to scale.
He said the bank had disbursed close to N30 billion over more than eleven years, financed teachers, private schools, local government employees, health-sector customers, agricultural value-chain operators and renewable energy operators.

POST: MICROFINANCE OPERATORS SHOULD KNOW DIFFERENCE BETWEEN REGULATORY CAPITAL AND BUSINESS CAPITAL
URL: https://www.mainstreetmfb.com/microfinance-operators-should-know-difference-between-regulatory-capital-and-business-capital-adegoke-adegbami-managing-director-ceo-mainstreet-microfinance-bank/
Date: November 19, 2020
Category: Interview
Exclusive interview with Adegoke Adegbami, Managing Director/CEO, Mainstreet Microfinance Bank.
He discussed the bank's achievements over the previous 12 months, including deployment of mobile banking, internet banking, debit cards, USSD service and correspondent banking.
He said the bank's customers could transact via Stanbic IBTC branches in real time and that the bank received a Bbb- rating from Agusto & Co.
He stated that in 2019 the bank opened a new branch in Ikorodu and held its 10-year anniversary.
He explained the need for microfinance banks to distinguish regulatory capital from business/operational capital, saying money is the stock in trade and institutions must continue to increase capital and funding.
He discussed investment grade rating, investor confidence, potential business groups, microfinance's role in SMEs, regulatory guidelines, awards and future projections.

POST: CREDIT-WORTHY CUSTOMERS BENEFIT MORE DURING FINANCIAL CRISIS - ADEGBAMI, CEO, MAINSTREET MFB
URL: https://www.mainstreetmfb.com/credit-worthy-customers-benefit-more-during-financial-crisis-adegbami-ceo-mainstreet-mfb/
Date: November 19, 2020
Category: Interview
Adegoke Adegbami discussed Mainstreet MFB's response to COVID-19, including provision of sanitizers, masks and gloves, customer and staff awareness, remote working, e-channel service delivery, and support through the National Association of Microfinance Banks, Lagos Chapter.
He said the Board approved a three-month moratorium on loan repayment for customers directly affected by COVID-19, without loan forgiveness or interest waiver, and without penalties where conditions are met.
He discussed moratorium misunderstandings, CBN interventions, BOI/DBN on-lending, NIRSAL MFB, government response, palliatives, SMEs, health sector weaknesses, diversification and advice for microfinance banks and SMEs to survive after COVID-19.

POST: A PEEK INTO OUR HISTORY
URL: https://www.mainstreetmfb.com/a-peek-into-our-history/
Date: January 7, 2019
Category: Press Release
Mainstreet Microfinance Bank, formerly Mainstreet Bank Microfinance Bank, would have operated for ten years in March 2019.
The bank is a private liability company incorporated and licensed by the Central Bank of Nigeria to provide microfinance banking services.
From incorporation in 2008 until December 2017, it was a subsidiary of a commercial bank until fully divested to an independent business entity.
The bank stated it was repositioning itself to serve customers better, especially through technological innovations.
Since incorporation, the bank says it has provided micro credits and other financial services to customers and businesses needing working capital and expansion funding across Lagos branches.
The article ends by referencing its strong brand, corporate governance, management and workforce, and its slogan: value to your world.

POST: AGUSTO & CO. ASSIGNS A “Bbb-“ RATING TO MAINSTREET MICROFINANCE BANK LIMITED
URL: https://www.mainstreetmfb.com/agusto-co-assigns-a-bbb-rating-to-mainstreet-microfinance-bank-limited/
Date: December 13, 2018
Category: News
The page states that the rating of Mainstreet Microfinance Bank is underpinned by good capitalisation, good profitability, satisfactory asset quality, satisfactory liquidity profile and a stable management team.
The rating is tempered by weak risk management practices around bank placements and manual loan approval and collection processes, as well as a narrow funding base that restricts the ability of the MFB to effectively grow its loan book.
