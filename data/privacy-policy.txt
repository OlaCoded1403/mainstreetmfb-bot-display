Mainstreet MFB Privacy Policy
Date shown: February 11, 2021
Mainstreet MFB (“we,” “our,” or “us”) is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how your personal information is collected, used, and disclosed by Mainstreet MFB.
The policy applies to the website, associated subdomains and the Mainstreet MFB application.
Definitions include Cookie, Company, Country, Customer, Device, IP address, Personnel, Personal Data, Service, Third-party service, Website and You.
Information collected includes information submitted when visiting the website, registering, placing an order, subscribing to the newsletter, responding to a survey or filling out a form.
Collected information includes name/username, phone numbers and email addresses.
The bank may receive some information from third parties when customers contact the bank, including automated fraud detection providers, and may collect information publicly available on social media websites.
The bank may share personal and non-personal information with third parties such as advertisers, contest sponsors, promotional/marketing partners, service providers, current and future affiliated companies and business partners, and successors-in-interest in the event of merger, asset sale or reorganisation.
Trusted third-party service providers may support hosting, server maintenance, database storage/management, email management, marketing, credit-card processing, customer service and product/service fulfillment.
The bank may share log data such as IP addresses for analytics purposes and may disclose information to government, law enforcement, courts or private parties where necessary to comply with legal obligations, protect rights or prevent illegal/unethical activity.
Information is used to personalize experience, improve the website, improve customer service, process transactions, administer contests/promotions/surveys/site features and send periodic emails.
Email addresses submitted through the website may be used to send communications, with unsubscribe options included in emails.
The bank states that information is kept only as long as needed to provide services and fulfil policy purposes, unless legal or regulatory obligations require retention.
Security measures include physical, electronic and managerial procedures, secure servers and SSL technology, though the bank states no security system can be guaranteed as foolproof.
Users may have rights to request updates or corrections depending on their relationship with Mainstreet MFB.
